# Week 2 Progress Report: Firmware Implementation and Hardware Testing

## Overview

Week 2 focused on firmware implementationamd hardware prototyping. An initial breadboard prototype consisting of an ESP32 controller and TMC2209 stepper motor drivers was developed. Additionally, FluidNC firmware was studied and configured to establish motion control for the PCB prototyping machine. Practical exposure to soldering iron circuitry and assembly was also gained during this period.

## Tasks Completed

- Developed and tested a breadboard prototype using ESP32 and TMC2209 drivers.
- Studied the configuration of FluidNC firmware.
- Successfully controlled and moved a stepper motor using FluidNC.
- Tested the motor control software developed by the software team.
- Assembled and tested six soldering iron units.
- Learned the working principles and circuitry of soldering irons.

## Challenges Faced

- Observed oscillation and vibration issues in the Y-axis stepper motor.
- Attempted multiple troubleshooting methods, including motor wire reconfiguration and driver testing, but the issue persisted.
- Soldering iron assembly required significant time and careful handling during manufacturing and testing.

## Key Outcomes

- Established a functional firmware-based motion control setup.
- Verified communication between hardware and control software.
- Improved understanding of embedded motion control systems and hardware assembly processes.

## Next Steps

- Continue troubleshooting and resolving the Y-axis motor vibration issue.
- Integrate and test the laser module with the existing control system.
- Further optimize FluidNC configuration for stable machine operation.
